Regional Analysis


This page provides invaluable insights into the grocery prices trends across different regions of Australia, including Adelaide, Perth, Sydney, Melbourne, Darwin, Canberra, Hobart, and Brisbane. By studying these regional differences, GDC aids to make informed decisions about where to shop or invest across all parts of Australia.


Key Insights


  • During COVID (2020), Adelaide had the highest positive percentage rate compared to the previous quarter.

  • Over the past five decades, Australia experienced its first decline in percentage change from the previous quarter in 2012, reaching a peak decrease of -8.3%.

  • For the past 10 years, among selected grocery categories, beef has shown the most significant price fluctuations, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic.

  • Fruits show the most significant price differences among all food categories across Australia's regions, with a variation of nearly 54%.


Australian Food and Non-Alcoholic Beverages Price Changes by State


This analysis focuses on how the prices of food and non-alcoholic beverages have changed across different states in Australia by percentage. The visualisation utilises percentage change from the previous year's quarter, with Melbourne's food prices changing by 15.3% recently in 2023. On the other hand, Perth had the highest number of instances with negative percentage changes in the years 2012, 2016, and 2017.
