CPI Forecasting


Welcome to GDC's CPI Forecasting page, where we dive into the fascinating world of consumer price trends in Australia's grocery sector. We aim to provide you with valuable insights into how CPI in general and specifically relating to food & non-alcoholic beverages are evolving over time. With the aim of helping consumers make well-informed decisions, GDC has committed to a rigourous forecasting methodology for Australian CPI movements.


Key Insights


  • GDC has utilise the XGBoost methodology to conduct forecasts on Australian Food & Non-Alcoholic Beverages and All Groups CPI data.

  • GDC has eliminated outliers and utilised cross validation techniques to accurately forecast CPI trends. This has enabled the smoothing out external market events which have caused unprecdented surges in CPI levels.

  • Food & Non-Alcoholic Beverages has generally oscillated between -2% to 4% in change from previous quarter for the last 30+ years. GDC has predicted an average quarterly change of 2.97% for the next 10 years.

  • The All Groups CPI has lower movement, oscillating between -1% to 2% each period for the last 30+ years. GDC has predicted an average quarterly change change between 0.62% and 0.69% for the next 10 years.


Forecasting Methodology


Our approach to forecasting the Consumer Price Index (CPI) relies on a powerful methodology known as XGBoost. This method stands out for its exceptional accuracy in predicting complex trends.

But what makes XGBoost so useful?

XGBoost, or Extreme Gradient Boosting, is an ensemble learning algorithm that combines the predictions of multiple models to create a robust and highly accurate forecast. It excels in capturing intricate relationships within data, making it ideal for predicting the CPI.

Why XGBoost?

You might be wondering why we chose XGBoost. The answer lies in its ability to analyze vast datasets and uncover hidden patterns. This empowers us to make precise predictions about grocery price movements. With XGBoost, we can navigate the complexities of the market, from seasonal fluctuations to supply and demand dynamics.
