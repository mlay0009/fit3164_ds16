Macro Outlook


An important economic indicator for Australia is the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which tracks changes in the average price level of a basket of goods and services used by households. For Australian households, this index is used to track inflation and gauge changes in the cost of living. GDC aims to provide insight into how macroeconomic factors have impacted the everyday consumer in relation to inflationary pressures.


Key Insights


  • The consumer price index (or CPI) for Australia decreased by 1.9% in the June 2020 quarter, the largest decrease in the index's 72-year history of monitoring (ABS).

  • The decline in CPI during the COVID-19 spending boom resulted in greater discretionary income for households.

  • In a post COVID economy, Australia has experienced dramatic increases in CPI, reporting an all time high annual change of 7.8% in Q4 of 2022.


Australian CPI Movement


GDC has enabled consumers to look at how CPI has changed over time on a quarterly and annual basis. This informs consumers on how their purchasing power has been impacted by wider economic factors such as inflation.



Australian Food Inflation


Inflation refers to the overall increase in the level of prices for goods and services over time in an economy. GDC is particularly concerned in analysing food inflation which refers to the rate of increase in food costs within the Australian economy. This visualisation offers insight into how Australia’s food and non-alcoholic beverages CPI category has moved in the last few years.
