Grocery Sub-Categories


This page will help you understand how different subcategories of food have been impacted over time. GDC has included statistics about price changes for categories in the grocery sector.


Key Insights


  • Australian grocery categories have followed a similar trend in the last three years, peaking in the second half of 2022 then slowly plateauing as inflation stabilises.

  • Since 2000 grocery prices for household items has slowly risen, with major price increases over 200% for some items.

  • Grocery item prices have also been heavily affected by supply related issues within Australia. For the June Quarter in 2023, Bread and cereal products have increased the most by 2.9% from the previous quarter.

  • For annual movements since June 2023, Dairy and related products have increased the most by 15.2% from last year.

Annual food inflation softens but remains high


On an annual basis, food and non-alcoholic beverages inflation eased to 7.5% in the June quarter, down from 8.0% in the March quarter and the peak of 9.2% in the December quarter.

Annual price increases were highest for:

  • Dairy products (15.2%)

  • bread and cereal products (11.6%)

  • Food products n.e.c (not elsewhere classified)(11.3%)


Price rises were softer for fresh food categories such as:


  • Meat and seafood (3.5%)

  • Fruit and vegetables (1.6%)




Quarterly food inflation has followed in line with annual movements


For the June Quarter, inflation for food and non-alcoholic beverages has eased to a 1.6% increase to last quarter.

Quarterly price increases were the highest for:


  • Bread and cereal products (2.9%)
  • Fruits and vegetables (2.4%)
  • Non-alcoholic beverages (2.2%)

Price rises were materially lower for meats and seafoods with only a 0.1% increase to last quarter.




Reference period for all visualisations: June Quarter 2023
